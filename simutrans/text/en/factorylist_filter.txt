<title>Industry List Help</title>

<h1><strong>Industry List</strong></h1>

<p>
<strong>Industry List</strong> shows information about all <a href="industry_info.txt">industries</a> (suppliers and consumers of goods) in the current game.
</p>
<p>
To open the <strong>Industry List</strong> window: click on the industry list icon in the <a href="list.txt">List Management</a> window.<br>
<em>Tip:</em> If items in list are only partially visible, then <a href="mouse.txt">re-size</a> the <strong>Industry List</strong> window or scroll the list using the slider bar.
</p>
<p>
<em>Sorted by:</em> has options to determine the order of the industries listd.<br>
Click on the buttons to cycle through the options (changes name of the option button):
</p>
<p>
- <em>Factory name</em> orders alphabetically by name.<br>
- <em>Input</em> orders by input storage capacity.<br>
- <em>Output</em> orders by output storage capacity.<br>
- <em>Production</em> orders by production capacity.<br>
- <em>Status</em> orders by status colour bar (see below).<br>
- <em>Power</em> orders by electricity supplied.<br>
- <em>Sector</em> orders by economic sectors.
</p>
<p>
- <em>ascending</em> / <em>descending</em> reverses the order of the list.
</p>
<p>
Click on an item listd in the <strong>Industry List</strong> window to see more <a href="industry_info.txt">information</a> about that industry. Click on the grey arrow next to each individual industry listd to be taken to the location of that industry in the main game window.
<br>Items listd for each industry include:
</p>
<p>
<em>Status colour bar:</em> colours indicate the status of the industry (also used in <a href="industry_info.txt">Industry Information</a>):<br>
- <em>White:</em> does not produce and consume.<br>
- <em>Green:</em> at optimum status.<br>
- <em>Dark skyblue:</em> at optimum status of marine resources.<br>
- <em>Cyan:</em> some of the lines are operating normally.<br>
- <em>Dark blue:</em> sufficient marine resources are remaining.<br>
- <em>Dark green:</em> sufficient materials are waiting for transportation.<br>
- <em>Gray:</em> not connected by goods transport.<br>
- <em>Dark brown:</em> production is stopped due to excessive inventory.<br>
- <em>Dark yellow:</em> can not operate because some materials are missing.<br>
- <em>Yellow:</em> can not operate/open because there is no material or goods at all.<br>
- <em>Orange:</em> since some items are overstocked, it stopped receiving the goods.<br>
- <em>Orange red:</em> since all items are overstocked, it stopped receiving.<br>
- <em>Red:</em> since all the storage is full, arrival and operation are stopped.<br>
<br>
- <em>Purple frame:</em> the production rate is declining due to the staff shortage.
</p>
<p>
A <em>Red lightning flash</em> is shown if the industry has an <a href="electricity.txt">electricity</a> supply.
</p>
<p>
<em>Factory name:</em> the name of the <a href="industry_info.txt">industry</a>.
</p>
<p>
The three numbers in brackets represent:<br>
<em>Current input:</em> the amount of goods stored ready to be processed.<br>
<em>Current output:</em> the amount of goods that are ready for transportation.<br>
<em>Total production:</em> the production rate of the factory.<br>
A dash ("-") is shown instead of a zero in each case.
</p>
